STEM Courses

Discover a range of beginner-friendly and advanced STEM courses designed to help students explore science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.


Science Fundamentals


Learn the basics of biology, chemistry, and physics through fun, interactive lessons designed for young learners.

  • Introduction to Biology
  • Chemistry Essentials
  • Earth & Space Science
  • Human Body Systems
  • Environmental Science Projects

Students participate in hands-on labs, simple experiments, and guided discovery activities that help build a strong foundation in scientific thinking, observation skills, and understanding of the natural world.

Technology & Coding


Explore programming and technology concepts with beginner-friendly projects using tools like Scratch and Python.

  • Scratch Programming Basics
  • Intro to Python Coding
  • Web Design With HTML/CSS
  • Robotics & Sensors
  • Safe & Responsible Technology Use

This course introduces students to logical thinking, step-by-step problem solving, computer safety, and creativity through a variety of beginner-friendly coding tools and interactive tech challenges.

Engineering Challenges


Put your problem-solving skills to the test with creative, hands-on engineering activities using everyday materials.

  • Simple Machines & Mechanical Design
  • Bridge-Building & Structural Engineering
  • Renewable Energy Systems
  • Water Filtration & Environmental Engineering
  • Engineering Design Process Workshops

Learners explore real-world engineering concepts through project-based activities that develop teamwork, creativity, and innovation. Students design, build, test, and iterate using accessible materials.

Mathematics in Action


Build confidence with numbers, patterns, and problem-solving through real-world math activities connected to STEM.

  • Algebra in the Real World
  • Geometry Through Art & Design
  • Introduction to Data & Statistics
  • Patterns, Sequences, & Logic
  • Financial Literacy for Students

This course helps students understand the power of math in everyday problem-solving. Through puzzles, STEM-based activities, and applied math challenges, learners build confidence and critical-thinking skills.
